\noindent \textbf{Problem 1. (3.0 points)} On the set $\mathbb{Z}$ of integers, define the operation $(*)$ as follows:
$$a * b = a + b + 2026 \quad \text{for all } a, b \in \mathbb{Z}.$$
\indent a) Prove that the set $\mathbb{Z}$ together with the operation $(*)$ forms a group.\\
\indent b) Is the set $X = \{4k + 2 \mid k \in \mathbb{Z}\}$ a subgroup of the group $(\mathbb{Z}, *)$? Justify your answer.

\vspace{0.3cm}
\noindent \textbf{Problem 2. (1.5 points)} Let $X = \langle x \rangle_m$ be a multiplicative cyclic group of order $m$ generated by $x$, and $Y = \langle y \rangle_n$ be a multiplicative cyclic group of order $n$ generated by $y$. Prove that the rule $\varphi: X \to Y; x^l \mapsto (y^k)^l$ ($k \in \mathbb{N}^*$) is a homomorphism when $km$ is divisible by $n$.

\vspace{0.3cm}
\noindent \textbf{Problem 3. (2.0 points)} Consider the set $\mathbb{Z}(\sqrt{2}) = \{a + b\sqrt{2} \mid a, b \in \mathbb{Z}\}$.\\
\indent a) Prove that $\mathbb{Z}(\sqrt{2})$ is a ring with the usual addition and multiplication of real numbers.\\
\indent b) Is the ring $\mathbb{Z}(\sqrt{2})$ a field? Justify your answer.

\vspace{0.3cm}
\noindent \textbf{Problem 4. (1.5 points)}\\
\indent a) State the definitions of a field and an integral domain.\\
\indent b) Prove that every field is an integral domain.\\
\indent c) For $n > 1$, is the ring $\mathbb{Z}^n$ an integral domain? Justify your answer.

\vspace{0.3cm}
\noindent \textbf{Problem 5. (2.0 points)}\\
\indent a) Use Eisenstein's criterion to prove that the polynomial $x^3 - 3x + 1$ is irreducible in $\mathbb{Q}[x]$.\\
\indent b) In the ring $\mathbb{Z}_7[x]$, consider the polynomials $f = x^3 + \overline{p}x + \overline{5}$ and $g = x^2 + \overline{5}x + \overline{6}$. Determine $p$ such that $f$ is divisible by $g$.
